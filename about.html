About Us

The story of three sisters who transformed their love of books into a movement to inspire young readers and strengthen their communities in Puerto Rico. Rooted in vision, planning, and a shared passion for the joy of learning.

Our Story

Tres Hermanas PR began with three sisters who loved books and wanted to share that love with children in Puerto Rico.

In 2017, our family moved to Aguadilla, Puerto Rico, just before Hurricanes Irma and Maria. After the storms, we spent months without electricity, water, or internet. What kept us going? Books. We read together, shared stories, and found comfort in the worlds that books opened for us.

But we quickly realized how few books were available to children around us. The local university library had only 80 children's books, and our school's small library reached only a handful of students. We missed the easy access to libraries and Little Free Libraries we had known in the Bay Area.

That's when we had an idea: why not bring Little Free Libraries to Aguadilla? In 2018, we built our first bookshelf in a local park and filled it with children's books. Kids came, read, borrowed, and shared. And we knew we were onto something.

Today's Mission

Today, Tres Hermanas PR is a youth inspired, youth led, and youth run organization with support from some dedicated adults. We collect new and gently used children's books from across the U.S. and ship them to Puerto Rico, stocking Little Free Libraries and community spaces where kids can discover the joy of reading.

Our mission is simple: to give children in Puerto Rico the same access to books that so many kids take for granted. Every book sent is a chance to spark imagination, learning, and hope.

Our Mission

We want to invite you to be part of something pretty cool - Sending books across the United States and over the Atlantic Ocean to put them in Little Free Libraries we are building in Puerto Rico. Organize a small book drive. You can go to your class, your Girl/Boy Scout Troop, your parents' place of work, even your public library. Tell them about your interest in this project and would they be interested too? If yes - you are only a little bit of work away from giving a kid a book.
